## PRODUCT OVERVIEW

The Compact Foldable RC Winger is a remote-controlled bird launcher purpose-built for retriever dog training — redesigned from the ground up to solve the single biggest frustration in the sport: a device that's too large and heavy to carry into the field without a dedicated haul system. Unlike every current market option, which folds flat and ships in bulky, awkward cases, this winger uses a camp-chair-style folding mechanism to collapse into a compact rectangular envelope that packs cleanly into a carry bag. At a target weight of 8 lbs or less — less than two-thirds of the lightest competitor — it's built to go where trainers go, covering terrain that current wingers simply can't reach. Designed for weatherproof, rust-resistant outdoor use in mud and water, compatible with Garmin and Dog Truck OEM electronic triggers, and capable of launching a 3–3.5 lb duck or dummy from 25 to 300 yards, this is the first retriever winger built as a carry device first and a launcher second.

## OBJECTIVES

FOLDING MECHANISM & STRUCTURAL ARCHITECTURE
• Design a camp-chair-style folding mechanism that collapses the winger into a compact rectangular packed envelope suitable for a carry bag
• Engineer the fold geometry, pivot points, and latch/lock system to withstand repeated throw-force cycles under field conditions
• Validate that the folded envelope and deployed configuration can co-exist within the weight target of 8 lbs or less

THROW ARM & LAUNCH SYSTEM
• Design a throw arm capable of launching a 3–3.5 lb duck or dummy across a 25–300 yard range
• Ensure the throw arm integrates cleanly with the folding mechanism without requiring disassembly between deployed and stowed positions
• Evaluate structural performance of the arm and pivot under repeated load cycles

ELECTRONICS INTEGRATION & HOUSING
• Design mounting provisions and housing architecture to accommodate Garmin and Dog Truck OEM electronic trigger modules
• Ensure wire management and cable routing are compatible with the folding structure — no pinch points, no exposed runs
• Confirm weatherproofing and sealed access for electronics compartment

MATERIALS & WEATHERPROOFING
• Explore material strategy to achieve the ≤8 lb weight target while meeting rust-resistance and weatherproof requirements for outdoor mud and water use
• Develop CMF direction that communicates durability and field-readiness
• Confirm hardware and fastener grades appropriate for corrosive outdoor environments

HERO SKU ARCHITECTURE & FUTURE SCALABILITY
• Design the hero SKU as the primary product while establishing a modular architecture that can accommodate 2–3 size variants in future development phases
• Develop a scalable structural framework so future SKUs do not require full redesign
